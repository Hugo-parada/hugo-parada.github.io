About

Hello, my name is Hugo Parada Ríos. I was born in San Bernardo, Chile. Since October 2025, I have been a postdoctoral researcher at Inria Nancy – Grand Est, in the team SPHINX, affiliated with the Institut Élie Cartan de Lorraine, under the supervision of Alessandro Duca and Rémi Buffe.

From October 2023 to September 2025, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institut de Mathématiques de Toulouse, under the supervision of Franck Boyer. I defended my PhD thesis at the Laboratoire Jean Kuntzmann in Grenoble, France, under the supervision of Emmanuelle Crépeau and Christophe Prieur. I completed an M.Sc. in Mathematics at Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María, under the supervision of Eduardo Cerpa and Patricio Guzmán.

Research

My research centers on the study of partial differential equations with a particular focus on control theory, including well-posedness, controllability, observability, and stabilization.

PDE control KdV and dispersive systems Coupled systems Graphs and networks Delays and saturation Geometric control Numerical methods Plasma physics

Main interests

  • Control problems for PDEs on graphs and network structures.
  • Stabilization of PDEs in the presence of delay, saturation, or disturbances.
  • Controllability and stabilization of coupled systems.
  • Geometric control and qualitative properties of evolution equations.
  • Numerical methods for kinetic equations and models from plasma physics.
